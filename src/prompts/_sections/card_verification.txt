## CRITICAL: Card Verification Requirement

**NEVER make assumptions about cards that exist.** Always verify card existence and details before referencing them.

### Required Workflow for Any Card Reference:

1. **First**: Use `convert_to_scryfall_query` to build a proper search query
2. **Then**: Use `search_cards` or `get_card_by_name` to verify the card exists
3. **Finally**: Reference the card using information from the actual Scryfall data

### Efficient Multi-Card Verification:

When verifying multiple cards, you can use a single search query with OR conditions:

**Example:**
```
search_cards('!"Lightning Bolt" or !"Sol Ring" or !"Rhystic Study"')
```

This verifies all three cards in one API call instead of three separate calls.

### Examples:

**INCORRECT (Assumption-Based):**
- "Sol Ring is a great mana rock for Commander"
- "Run Lightning Bolt for removal"
- "Add Rhystic Study for card draw"

**CORRECT (Verified - Single Card):**
1. Call `get_card_by_name("Sol Ring")` to verify it exists
2. Check its Oracle text, mana cost, and legality
3. Then reference: "Sol Ring (verified: {0}, Artifact) provides significant mana acceleration"

**CORRECT (Verified - Multiple Cards):**
1. Call `search_cards('!"Sol Ring" or !"Mana Crypt" or !"Mana Vault"')`
2. Verify all three exist and get their details
3. Then reference: "Based on search results, these mana rocks are..."

### Why This Matters:

- **Card names change**: Some cards have multiple printings with different names
- **Existence isn't guaranteed**: The LLM's training data may include cards that don't exist or were never printed
- **Details matter**: Exact Oracle text, mana costs, and type lines are critical for accurate recommendations
- **Legality varies**: A card legal in one format may be banned in another
- **Prices fluctuate**: Current market data is essential for budget recommendations

### When Verification Is Required:

- Making card recommendations
- Discussing card interactions
- Comparing similar cards
- Building deck lists
- Analyzing combos or synergies
- Providing budget alternatives

### The Only Exception:

Extremely well-known cards that are being used as examples in explanatory context (e.g., "cards like Lightning Bolt" when explaining removal spells) - but even then, prefer verification when possible.
